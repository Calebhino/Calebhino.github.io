Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is the ability for your brain to change through practice. As you repeat tasks the connections in the brain begin to strengthen and can form new pathways.

Understanding neuroplasticity is beneficial because it illustrates that practice is the important factor in achieving what you want and opens people up to become better versions of themselves.

Personally I can apply my understanding of neuroplasticity to my work where I feel frustrated, scared or inadequate by focusing on starting something with the understanding that the more I do the better I will become. I can also use this to tackle imposter syndrome because I can practice focusing on the actual practice instead of worrying about failing. Over time I hope to encourage failure as it allows me to grow.

You can increase your neuroplasticity by tackling new challenges or crafts and forming new pathways throughout the brain. Some examples include: learning and instrument, painting or drawing, meditation, and more.

Growth Mindset

The growth mindset builds off of the research of neuroplasticity and is a way of approaching problems and thinking in general. It is about having an understanding that our minds are malleable and that with practice we can grow and become more equipped to tackle certain problems.

Nothing really surprised me during my exploration as it felt confirming in my personal understanding and my personal frustrations with the education system I was raised in.

I will integrate the growth mindset into my learning as discussed above, this mindset is building off the understanding of neuroplasticity. As I continue with the dev academy course I can apply this mindset and focus on repetition and practice with the skills that I struggle with.

Effect on my learning plan

With this understanding I can approach problems along my journey as a piece of the journey rather then something to tick off or achieve. In my learning plan I talked about working on something everyday, and above I talked about just starting something and focusing on practicing even if it is small amounts. This is always something I have struggled with as I normally leave my work to the last minute as I developed habits of minimum effort maximum reward during school which allowed me to be super lazy. During this course I hope to practice and focus more on the journey and develop my skills rather than do the bare minimum.
