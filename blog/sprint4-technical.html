Problem Solving

A time I got blocked on a simple problem

During last sprints FreeCodeCamp challenges and I got to the recursion section. I’ve used recursion before and find it incredibly elegant in its nature but it can also bloody complicated. Recursion is another way that we can run a bit of code over and over again. Having not used this method in a little while I got caught up with a problem when running the solution I had come up with. I spent a while thinking about the issue and why it wasn’t happening. I started googling to look at some other examples that I could model to see if mine was in line with others, I also used console.log to check my code at different points, and even used the rubber ducky technique and talked to someone else about my problem for a little bit. I eventually realised that I was missing a very simple bit of code that meant my solution wouldn’t run forever. I felt pretty frustrated throughout the process as I was pretty confident in my recursion ability but was frustrated with my inability to solve the problem. Once I figured out my silly mistake I was chuffed. I learnt sometimes you just need to go back to basics when trying to solve a problem, even if you think you should be able to solve it without.

A time I solved a problem in an elegant way

During this sprints “Built-in-Methods” I was writing a piece of code that would take a series of words and capitalize the first letter of each of them. To solve this problem I used the hints provided and googled some premade tools that I could include in my solution to manipulate the words, when I wasn’t sure why it wasn’t working I would use console.log to let me know what was happening at each point. Throughout the process I felt focused and in flow because I was able to breeze through it quickly and get fast feedback that allowed me to solve the problems. Once I finished I sat back and thought “wow”, I was impressed with how small my solution was, but also at how it was quite complex. I learnt how to compress my code a bit more then I might have initially done.
