End of Foundations Reflections

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

  1. I'm a bit of a perfectionist. I've found that when I am coding i try to find the most efficient and simple solution possible, but sometimes this can cause me to procrastinate
  2. I can be a bit egotistical about my ability with programming and get frustrated when I can't figure something out
  3. Stillness is a real challenge for me. I always like to be doing something, and get bored really quickly.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

Values, empathy and self awareness are all essential to learning and programming. Values and empathy help define how a person interprets information and approaches problems, and self awareness allows a person to observe and acknowledge behaviours and make changes accordingly for the betterment of oneself. These are all useful in programming because there is an incredible social aspect to working in a team, and diversity of differences in peoples values, empathy levels, etc. allow for people to approach problems differently.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Even though some of the stuff felt quit wishwashing it wasn't difficult to understand, in fact it felt like it confirmed a lot of my experiences and it felt like it made sense. Because of this it made me a little bit frustrated with previous education systems i've been through as I have always felt they were inadequate and didn't enjoy school other then the social elements.

I also found it difficult sometimes to sit and try and write down what was happening in my head as it never felt accurate upon rereading and this was suprising because I've never really been asked to do that before while learning.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I found commiting to the meditation and mindfullness difficult as doing this part of the course self paced there wasn't a structure that forced me to do it which made it difficult.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

Because theres quite a reputation of programmers lacking social skills in professional environments and because it is incredibly important for a team to be able to communicate properly.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practising programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don't think it was a waste of time, I did enjoy it less than the programming but I gained a lot of value out of it, even though I found it difficult at times to write things out.
