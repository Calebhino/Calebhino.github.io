My Personal Values, Ethics and Strengths

About my values

The most important ethical decision in my life happened a few years ago when leaving high school. I knew I wanted to be successful and I wanted to enjoy what I do, but I wasn't entirely drawn to one path. When I was younger I wanted to be an inventor and make things, so I was interested in engineering, but then my brother became an engineer and It made it seem mundane and uninteresting. Then I wanted to be a film maker because I've always loved making films and it was something I was passionate about, there just wasn't a great success rate and the path wasnt clear, especially because I felt the education options were lackluster. Then I started looking at Computer Science. I knew I loved building websites and doing graphic design in my own time, and i rocked at Computer Science in school (subtle brag). I decided I wanted to study Computer Graphics (Computer Science but with a focus on animation and such) at Victoria University. I has my eye on Victoria University for a long time, I had a desire to go live somewhere new that had a vibrant culture. During that time I made some pretty big personal revelations. I realised I really wasn't happy with the person I was and I didn't feel like the people I was around would help be the person I wanted to be. Because of this I focused on meeting new people at university and met my best friends, I also became really interested in psychology and went to study that at University alongside Computer Graphics. I started reading a bunch of self help books and finding a few new communities. I had reached a turning point and I almost entirely deconstructed my value and belief systems and built them back up. I started to assume I was always wrong with the intention of learning and growing. This idea has a few flaws but it worked for the most part. Because of this "rebirth" it set me on the path to become involved in a social enterprise that I fell in love with. I learnt about systems thinking and a tremendous amount about who I am as a person. I learnt about giftedness, self managed organisations and a bunch more stuff. It accelerated my growth by at least 5 years and it made me realise i want to creating things for the betterment of others and that I want to solve complex problems.

For a long time I didn't believe I actually had a culture because it wasnt something ever really talked about and what I was taught at school about other cultures seemed entirely seperate from my reality. In the past few years during a lot of self evaluation I realised that my privelige and upbringing influenced who I used to be quite heavily and have found that since then finding others with varying opinions and experiences has influenced who I am today and helped me to understand who I am.

My strengths and limitations

Whenever I do the Myer's Briggs test it alternates between Campaigner and Mediator with the main difference being introversion/extroversion.

The strengths listed under mediator are:

  • Thoughtful
  • Generous
  • Open-Minded
  • Creative
  • Passionate
  • Loyal to Their Values



I would say that I agree with most of these strengths and have found that they allow me to provide a different perspective then others in professional situations. In my last project I found that I became a voice of ethics among members of our team. I also found that my open-mindedness allowed me to learn and adapt quickly which led me to an incrdible amount of personal and professional growth.

The Weaknesses/Limitations listed under mediator are:

  • Overly Idealistic
  • Self-critical
  • Impractical
  • Emotionally Driven
  • Conflict-Averse
  • Difficult to Get to Know



There are a few limitations I don't particularly agree with and some I think can be strengths. However I do agree with the self critical limiation and I've found my self critical nature to be quite destructive in professional and personal environments. I can be incredibly Idealistic which sometimes leads to impractical ideas, but I have found that a lot of the time my creativity allows me to find practical solutions that work and often the impractical nature is deemed by other people.



I think that my creativity, open-mindedness and generous values will help me in my learning journey as they will allow me to work well with others while also problem solving solutions and taking in new ideas easily.



During my time working at Kete Kai (the social enterprise mentioned earlier) I spent a lot of time thinking about how as a team we could work better together. I proposed a lot of different ideas and was often supported but then nothing came of it because It wasn't deemed a priority. There was a lot of hostility between others in the team. One of the strategies that we tried later on in our working together was a system called Deep Democracy. Deep Democracy is a method that allows you to come to decisions while acknowledging and learning about other individuals perspectives while also adapting the decision so that the minority vote holds less hostility and can come along with the made decision.
In the future I would continue to refine my experience with Deep Democracy while also designing more self managed systems of resolving conflict and working together. There were many ideas i had and didn't get to try and implement and one day I would like to do so because I believe in building systems that work for people and I believe that the systems we have in place in most traditional organisations are not sufficient and are not designed with people in mind.
